Operating principle of the device

The device consists of a system for detecting and classifying garbage in the sea, differentiating it between plastic, metal and glass material by means of an infrared sensor which will emit light towards the bodies at sea and will measure the return of light in these materials and by means of a The ESP32 card will process the data and send it to the fire base platform and it will send the information to the SEEA page which will show the information classified.

Project phases

The first phase of the project is focused locally which will allow the device to be placed on the docks of the Ahome beaches and the Topolobampo port to determine and locate the different types of waste (metal, plastic, glass) for a short distance detection. The second phase consists in integrating the device into an underwater drone which will be deployed in the bay of Ohuira. The third phase consists in implementing this underwater drone throughout the Gulf of California and the last and last phase in any of the oceans of the planet. In addition to this there is the possibility of implementing them to ships of all types and fishing boats for the detection and location of waste (pastures, metal and glass) on its way.

Construction Material

The device will be constructed of carbon fiber because carbon fiber is a thermostable material that has a high resistance to temperature variations combined with a low density which is summarized in a lower weight and therefore a greater practicality to integrate the device into maritime means of transport which range from submarines to barges cargo ships.
